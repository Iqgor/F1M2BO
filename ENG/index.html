Rijksmuseum

Digital route

Emile, 1 Star

The museum has a great collection, but is wheelchair unfriendly. I have a normal wheelchair but just barely fit in the elevator. There is hardly any indication where the lifts are and which ones you need to get where you need to be. on the outside there is a curb all around with no rides. with a manual wheelchair that is doom but not with an electric wheelchair. The coffee corner is not accessible and a taxi van is not allowed to come close, so you have to get out a street further and pick up the same problem, especially because you don't know where the minibus is waiting for you. al emt al the Rijksmuseum is wheelchair unfriendly and not as stated is handicap accessible. it seems that the Rijksmuseum prefers not to have wheelchair users inside. Too bad for a museum that has been adapted with government money.

Joris, 3 Stars

The building was super beautiful! It is without a doubt the most beautiful museum in the Netherlands, so it is highly recommended. This collection contains only highlights and every piece of art is beautiful. You can easily walk around here for hours, if not days, without getting bored for a second, it was that interesting. Only it was a bit unclear where was what, that's a big minus, that is - 1 star. The other downside are the excruciatingly long lines, I myself did not have to wait long as a man, but my wife had to wait a whopping 30+ min! And we wanted to eat something from the restaurant, only the line was so long, so we went home to have something to eat. Other than that it was a great experience!
